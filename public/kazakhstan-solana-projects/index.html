7 Emerging Solana Projects from Kazakhstan You Should Watch in 2026

Deep dive into 7 promising Solana projects building in Kazakhstan: BalanceX, Lifted Finance, Apartchain, Yield AI, AiSol, SolPoint, and Metaforra.

OS
Pi Stack Team Editorial Team

The Central Asian crypto scene is exploding, and Kazakhstan has quietly become a Solana powerhouse. While most Western attention focuses on US and EU builders, a cluster of Kazakh projects are shipping real products on Solana—from DeFi yield optimizers to AI-powered trading agents.

Here is a deep dive into 7 projects from the Kazakhstan Solana ecosystem that deserve your attention right now.

1. BalanceX: Cross-Chain Yield Optimizer

BalanceX is a yield aggregation protocol that automatically routes liquidity across Solana and EVM-compatible chains. What makes it unique is its “smart rebalancing” engine that monitors APY spreads in real-time and moves capital without manual intervention.

Why it matters: Kazakh retail investors have historically been fragmented between CEX yield products and local DeFi. BalanceX bridges this gap with a one-click interface.

2. Lifted Finance: Social Trading for Solana

Lifted Finance brings copy-trading to Solana with a twist: it uses on-chain reputation scores to rank traders, rather than just raw ROI. This filters out lucky gamblers from consistent performers.

Why it matters: Social trading is saturated on Ethereum, but Solana’s low fees make micro-copy-trading ($5 minimums) viable for emerging markets.

3. Apartchain: Modular L2 Framework

Despite the name, Apartchain is building a Solana-native modular execution layer. It allows developers to spin up custom rollups that settle back to Solana’s mainnet, inheriting its liquidity and security.

Why it matters: As Solana’s congestion issues resurface during peak demand, modular scaling becomes critical. Apartchain’s approach is similar to Eclipse but optimized for emerging market use cases.

4. Yield AI: Machine Learning for DeFi Yields

Yield AI scrapes on-chain data from Raydium, Orca, and Meteora to predict which liquidity pools will outperform over the next 72 hours. It uses a lightweight transformer model trained on 18 months of Solana DEX data.

Why it matters: Most yield predictors are backward-looking. Yield AI’s forward-looking predictions give liquidity providers a statistical edge.

5. AiSol: AI-Powered Solana Smart Contract Auditor

AiSol is an automated smart contract scanner that uses LLMs to detect reentrancy, oracle manipulation, and access control vulnerabilities in Solana programs (Rust-based). It integrates directly with Anchor CLI for pre-deployment checks.

Why it matters: Solana’s rapid developer onboarding has led to a spike in exploited contracts. AiSol provides a free, open-source first line of defense.

6. SolPoint: Multi-Sig Treasury Management

SolPoint is a corporate treasury management tool for Solana. It supports multi-sig workflows, automated payroll in USDC, and integration with local Kazakh banking rails for fiat off-ramps.

Why it matters: Crypto companies in Kazakhstan need compliant treasury tools that bridge Solana and traditional finance. SolPoint fills this B2B gap.

7. Metaforra: On-Chain Real Estate Tokenization

Metaforra tokenizes commercial real estate in Almaty and Astana as SPL tokens. Investors can buy fractional ownership in rental properties, with rent distributed automatically via Solana smart contracts.

Why it matters: Real estate tokenization is the “killer app” for emerging markets with high inflation. Metaforra’s legal framework is already approved by Kazakh regulators.

Conclusion

The Kazakhstan Solana ecosystem is not just copying Western trends—it is solving specific problems for Central Asian markets: high inflation, fragmented liquidity, and limited access to global DeFi.

Whether you are a developer, investor, or DeFi user, watching these projects will give you an edge in spotting the next wave of Solana adoption.
